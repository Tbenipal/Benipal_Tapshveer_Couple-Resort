Algonquin Park

Summary of All-Inclusive

Algonquin Park, Ontario's first provincial park, offers a retreat from urban noise and chaos, preserving the natural beauty of maple and pine forests, inhabited by moose and wolves. Established in 1893, the park spans 7,500 square kilometers, showcasing a unique ecosystem shaped by glaciers thousands of years ago. Located northeast of Toronto and west of Ottawa, Algonquin attracts visitors seeking solace in its rugged landscape of rock formations and spring-fed lakes. The park, visible from the lakeshore of The Couples Resort, boasts pristine air, vibrant forests, and numerous lakes, providing a serene escape. With over 30 species of mammals, Algonquin offers opportunities to witness wildlife, including the haunting howl of wolves, and serves as a rare glimpse into Ontario's wilderness heritage.

Algonquin Park Activities

Summary of All-Inclusive

Algonquin Park offers a diverse range of experiences, with 17 hiking trails catering to all levels of ability, fishing, canoeing opportunities ranging from leisurely paddles to challenging portages, and abundant wildlife including loons and majestic moose. Visitors can explore the Visitor Centre for information and activities, as well as enjoy the Art Centre and Logging Museum, and the Algonquin Art Gallery. Just 15 minutes from the Bear Trail Couples Resort, the impressive $10 million Visitor Centre and Gallery, constructed in 1993 to commemorate Algonquin's centennial, showcases world-class exhibits on the park's natural and human history. With its historical and archaeological significance, Algonquin has inspired numerous literary, scientific, and artistic works, providing ample opportunities for visitors to appreciate its natural beauty while engaging in recreational activities.

Galeairy Lake

Summary of All-Inclusive

The Couples Resort sits on the picturesque shores of Galeairy Lake, offering direct access to Algonquin Park. Originally named Long Lake, it was later renamed Galeairy Lake due to its proximity to Nightengale and Airy townships. Spanning 11 kilometers (7 miles), Galeairy Lake is landlocked, primarily situated within the boundaries of Algonquin Park. Visitors can embark on adventures by canoe or kayak, exploring the tranquil coves while keeping an eye out for loons, beavers, otters, and majestic moose. Relaxing picnics on the shore and refreshing swims in the crystal-clear waters are a delightful way to spend the day.
